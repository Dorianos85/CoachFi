## CORE TOPICS

### 1. Emergency Fund (Fundusz awaryjny / 緊急資金)
- Target: 3–6 months of living expenses in a liquid, separate account.
- Start small: even saving 500 PLN / $100 / ¥15,000 is the first step.
- Keep it in a high-yield savings account, not invested — it must be accessible immediately.
- Polish context: PKO BP, mBank, Santander offer savings accounts with 4–6% interest (2024).
- Key message: "Your emergency fund is your financial seatbelt."

### 2. Budgeting — 50/30/20 Rule
- 50% Needs: rent, food, utilities, transport
- 30% Wants: entertainment, restaurants, subscriptions
- 20% Savings & debt repayment
- Adaptation: in high cost-of-living cities, use 60/20/20.
- Tool: track expenses for 30 days before budgeting.

### 3. Inflation (Inflacja / インフレ)
- Inflation erodes purchasing power: 1000 PLN today ≠ 1000 PLN in 10 years.
- At 5% annual inflation, money loses ~40% of value in 10 years.
- Solution: don't hold large cash reserves — invest in assets that outpace inflation.
- Inflation-beating assets: index funds, ETFs, real estate, TIPS (Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities).
- Polish CPI 2023: ~10%. Eurozone 2023: ~5.4%.

### 4. Compound Interest (Procent składany / 複利)
- "The eighth wonder of the world" — Einstein (attributed).
- Formula: A = P × (1 + r/n)^(nt)
- Example: 10,000 PLN at 8% annual return for 30 years = ~100,627 PLN.
- Key insight: time matters more than amount. Starting at 25 vs 35 can double the outcome.
- Rule of 72: divide 72 by annual return rate to estimate years to double money. (72 ÷ 8% = 9 years)

### 5. Index Funds & ETFs
- Index fund: tracks a market index (e.g., S&P 500, WIG20).
- ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund): like an index fund but traded on a stock exchange.
- Advantages: low fees (0.03–0.5% TER), diversification, passive management.
- Recommended for beginners: broad market ETFs (e.g., MSCI World, S&P 500 ETF).
- Polish options: XTB, BOSSA offer ETF trading with low fees.
- Never: pick individual stocks as a beginner. Start with broad market index funds.

### 6. Debt Management
- Two strategies:
  - **Avalanche**: pay off highest interest rate debt first (mathematically optimal).
  - **Snowball**: pay off smallest balance first (psychologically motivating).
- Credit card debt: typical APR 18–24%. Pay in full monthly — never carry a balance.
- Good debt vs bad debt: mortgage at 5% = potentially good. Credit card at 22% = always bad.
- Never take a loan to invest in volatile assets (crypto, stocks).

### 7. Credit Score (Historia kredytowa / 信用スコア)
- Poland: BIK (Biuro Informacji Kredytowej) scoring system.
- Factors: payment history (35%), credit utilization (30%), length of history (15%), types of credit (10%), new inquiries (10%).
- Improve score: pay bills on time, keep credit utilization below 30%, don't close old accounts.
- Check your BIK report annually for free at bik.pl.

### 8. Retirement Planning (Emerytura / 退職計画)
- Poland: ZUS (state pension) + PPK (Pracownicze Plany Kapitałowe) + IKE/IKZE (individual pension accounts).
  - IKE 2024 limit: 23,472 PLN/year (tax-exempt on withdrawal at 65+).
  - IKZE 2024 limit: 9,388.80 PLN/year (tax deduction now, taxed at 10% on withdrawal).
- USA: 401(k) + IRA (Roth IRA tax-free growth).
- Japan: iDeCo (individual defined contribution pension).
- Rule of thumb: save 15% of gross income for retirement from age 25+.
- Target retirement number: 25× your annual expenses (4% safe withdrawal rate).

### 9. Saving Habits (Nawyki oszczędzania / 貯蓄習慣)
- Pay yourself first: automate savings transfer on payday before spending.
- Even 10% of income saved consistently outperforms sporadic large savings.
- 30-day rule: wait 30 days before non-essential purchases over 200 PLN/$50.
- Zero-based budgeting: assign every PLN a job. Income − All Expenses = 0.

### 10. Net Worth (Majątek netto / 純資産)
- Net Worth = Assets − Liabilities
- Assets: cash, investments, real estate, pension value
- Liabilities: mortgage balance, loans, credit card debt
- Track monthly — growth trend matters more than absolute number.
- Median net worth at 30 in Poland: ~120,000–180,000 PLN (varies widely).

## QUICK RESPONSES BY TOPIC

**"How much should I save?"**
→ Start with 10–20% of net income. If that's impossible, start with 1%. Habit matters more than amount.

**"Is crypto a good investment?"**
→ Crypto is highly speculative and volatile — not suitable as a primary investment. If you choose to invest, limit to max 5% of your portfolio and only money you can afford to lose completely.

**"Should I pay off debt or invest?"**
→ If debt interest rate > 6–7%: pay debt first. If < 6%: consider splitting — some to debt, some to investing. Always keep emergency fund regardless.

**"I have 1000 PLN to invest — what do I do?"**
→ First ensure you have an emergency fund. Then open an IKE/IKZE account and buy a broad market ETF (e.g., MSCI World). Don't try to time the market.

**"What is the 50/30/20 rule?"**
→ 50% of income for needs (rent, food, bills), 30% for wants (fun, restaurants), 20% for savings and debt. Adjust as needed for your situation.

**"How do I start saving when I have no money?"**
→ Track every expense for 14 days — most people find 10–15% leakage on non-essentials. Cut one subscription. Automate 50 PLN transfer on payday. Small consistent action beats big irregular effort.
